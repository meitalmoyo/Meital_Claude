# LinkedIn Content — ch04: Tone & Voice

How Meital sounds on LinkedIn. For mediator brand content, Israeli audience.

---

## Core Voice: Warm Professional

Not cold and corporate. Not casual and slangy. The sweet spot:
- **Warm** — you feel like you're talking to a person, not a brand
- **Grounded** — confident without being arrogant
- **Clear** — ideas expressed simply, no jargon
- **Personal** — uses real experiences and observations, not generic advice
- **Direct** — Israeli-style dugri communication, no padding

---

## Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Sounds Like | Does Not Sound Like |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Warm | "אני מבינה שזה קשה" | Clinical distance |
| Confident | "לדעתי, המפתח הוא..." | "אולי, אם אפשר, כדאי..." |
| Grounded | Real examples, specific | Abstract platitudes |
| Direct | Short sentences, clear point | Overqualified hedging |
| Curious | Questions, openness | "הנה האמת" |

---

## What "Professional" Means Here

Professional does NOT mean:
- Formal language (לשון רשמית)
- Avoiding emotions
- Always having the answer
- Distancing yourself from the audience

Professional DOES mean:
- Showing up consistently
- Sharing real expertise
- Being accountable and honest
- Not oversharing or complaining

---

## Hebrew Register

**Use:** מדוברת מקצועית — spoken professional Hebrew. Not academic, not slang.

**Contractions and natural speech patterns:** Welcome. "אני רוצה" not "הנני מעוניין."

**Mix Hebrew and English:** Natural when appropriate. Technical terms (BATNA, mediation, LinkedIn) can stay in English. Don't over-translate what doesn't need translating.

**Avoid:**
- Formal legal Hebrew (sounds like a contract)
- Heavy anglicization (too many English words mid-sentence)
- Grammatical mistakes that undercut credibility — proofread

---

## Emotional Range

LinkedIn posts can include:
- **Curiosity** — "I've been wondering about this..."
- **Enthusiasm** — genuine excitement about mediation, learning
- **Honesty about difficulty** — "this isn't easy, but..."
- **Gentle challenge** — "have you considered that..."
- **Warmth** — care for the audience's experience

LinkedIn posts should NOT include:
- Anger or frustration at clients/institutions
- Complaints about the field or the process
- Oversharing personal struggles unrelated to the professional journey
- Sarcasm (loses something in text, especially cross-culturally)

---

## Sentence Rhythm

**Short sentences land harder.** After a longer explanation, one short sentence creates emphasis.

> ניהול סכסוך הוא לא כישרון שנולדים איתו.
> אפשר ללמוד את זה.
> כולם.

**Vary sentence length** — all-short feels staccato, all-long feels academic. Mix them.

---

## Voice Checklist Before Posting

- [ ] Does this sound like Meital, or like "a LinkedIn professional"?
- [ ] Is the first line interesting enough to stop the scroll?
- [ ] Did I make one clear point, not five?
- [ ] Is there something the reader can take away or think about?
- [ ] Did I use white space to make it easy to read?
- [ ] Is the Hebrew natural and correct?
