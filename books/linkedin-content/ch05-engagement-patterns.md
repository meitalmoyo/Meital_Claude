# LinkedIn Content — ch05: Engagement Patterns

What gets reactions, comments, and shares. To be updated after every post.

---

## What LinkedIn's Algorithm Rewards

1. **Comments** — weighted highest. A post with 10 comments outperforms one with 100 likes.
2. **Reactions** — likes, loves, etc.
3. **Shares** — with commentary perform better than silent reshares
4. **Dwell time** — how long people stop on your post (long posts that get read = good signal)
5. **Early engagement** — the first 60–90 minutes after posting matter most

**Implication:** Write posts that invite comments, not just passive reading. Ask a question. Take a position people want to respond to.

---

## What Drives Comments (Israeli LinkedIn)

- Asking a direct question at the end
- Taking a slightly counterintuitive position
- Personal vulnerability that others recognize
- Practical advice that sparks "I'd add..."
- Calling out a common misconception

---

## What Drives Shares

- Genuinely useful information people want to pass on
- Content that makes people look smart or thoughtful by sharing it
- Practical tips that feel "too good not to share"

---

## What Kills Engagement

- Generic content that could have been written by anyone
- No clear point or takeaway
- Starting with a weak hook (algorithm shows it to fewer people if first readers don't engage)
- Posting and disappearing — respond to every comment, especially in the first hour

## Responding to Comments

**Always respond.** Each response counts as additional engagement and keeps the post in the feed.

**Good responses:**
- Add something new ("exactly, and I'd also add...")
- Ask a follow-up question to continue the conversation
- Thank with substance ("that's a great point, because...")

**Avoid:**
- "Thanks!" alone (low value, kills the thread)
- Ignoring challenging comments — address them respectfully

---

## Building an Audience (Long Game)

- **Comment on others' posts** — thoughtful comments on relevant posts (mediation, negotiation, communication) introduce you to their audiences
- **Follow and engage with** Israeli mediators, conflict practitioners, HR professionals, lawyers — your future referral network
- **Consistency** — LinkedIn rewards regular posters. Showing up weekly is more important than viral posts.
- **Connect with everyone you meet** — classes, workshops, supervision sessions — LinkedIn is a relationship graph
