# LinkedIn Content — ch06: Posting Rhythm

Frequency, timing, and consistency strategy.

---

## Starting Out

**Goal for first 3 months:** 1 post per week, consistently. Not more.

Why not more? Quality beats quantity at the start. One strong post per week builds the habit, gives you time to respond to comments, and doesn't burn you out. You can increase later.

---

## Best Times to Post (Israeli LinkedIn)

LinkedIn is a work tool — people use it during work hours.

**Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
**Best times:** 
- Morning: 07:30–09:00 (commute / start of day)
- Midday: 12:00–13:30 (lunch break)
- Evening: 17:00–19:00 (after work)

**Avoid:** Friday afternoon, Saturday (Shabbat), Sunday morning (slow)

**Note:** Sunday is a workday in Israel — LinkedIn is more active Sunday than Saturday. Post Sunday if your weekly schedule makes Tuesday–Thursday hard.

---

## Content Planning (Monthly)

Plan one month ahead in themes, not exact posts. Flexibility within structure.

**Monthly template:**
| Week | Pillar | Format |
|------|--------|--------|
| Week 1 | Practical Tip | Numbered list |
| Week 2 | Personal Story | Story format |
| Week 3 | Education | Short insight |
| Week 4 | Perspective | Opinion post |

Rotate pillars so you're not always posting the same type.

---

## The First 60 Minutes Rule

After posting:
1. Stay near your phone
2. Respond to every comment within the first hour
3. Like and engage with 3–5 other posts (drives traffic back to your profile)
4. Don't edit the post in the first hour if possible (edits may reset the algorithm distribution)

---

## Batch Writing

Don't write posts the day you post them. Write in batches:
- Set aside 1–2 hours per week to write 2–3 posts
- Schedule ahead or keep a draft buffer
- Idea capture is separate from writing — keep a running list of ideas in notes

**Idea sources:**
- Something you learned in class this week
- A question a client or friend asked you
- A moment in your workday that surprised you
- A book passage or quote that made you think
- Something you read that you disagreed with

---

## When You Miss a Week

Don't double-post to "catch up." Just resume next week. Consistency is a long game — one missed week matters less than burning out and going silent for a month.
